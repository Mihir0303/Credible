2024 Elections: Key States Show Tight Race Between Ruling Coalition and Opposition

By Rajesh Kumar | New Delhi | Updated: June 12, 2023 08:32 IST

NEW DELHI: With less than a year to go for the 2024 general elections, the political landscape in India appears increasingly competitive, according to the latest surveys conducted by The Hindu across six key states.

The ruling National Democratic Alliance (NDA) and the opposition Indian National Developmental Inclusive Alliance (INDIA) are locked in a tight race, with the surveys indicating a margin of error of ±3%.

State-wise Breakdown

In Uttar Pradesh, which sends 80 members to the Lok Sabha, the NDA is projected to secure 42% of the vote share, while the INDIA bloc is close behind at 38%. The remaining 20% is divided among regional parties and others.

Maharashtra shows a different picture, with the ruling Mahayuti alliance leading at 45%, compared to the MVA's 40%. The state's complex political dynamics, following splits in major parties, have created uncertainty in many constituencies.

West Bengal continues to show dominance by the Trinamool Congress, which is projected to win 32 of the state's 42 seats. The BJP is expected to secure 8 seats, with the Left-Congress alliance managing 2.

In Tamil Nadu, the DMK-led alliance is projected to maintain its stronghold with 35 of the 39 seats, while the AIADMK-BJP alliance is expected to win 4 seats.

Key Issues Influencing Voters

According to the survey, unemployment remains the primary concern for 32% of voters, followed by price rise (28%), and development (22%). Corruption and national security were cited by 12% and 6% of respondents respectively.

"The employment situation, especially among youth, is the most significant factor in this election," said Dr. Meena Sharma, political analyst at the Centre for Policy Research. "Parties that can present a credible jobs plan will have an advantage."

The survey also indicates that welfare schemes continue to play a crucial role, with 68% of beneficiaries stating that these programs influence their voting decisions.

Regional Variations

In southern states, linguistic and cultural identity politics remain significant, while in the Hindi heartland, religious polarization continues to influence voter behavior.

The survey notes that women voters are increasingly making independent choices, with 62% of female respondents stating that they do not necessarily follow the voting preferences of male family members.

Voting patterns also vary significantly between urban and rural areas, with urban voters prioritizing infrastructure and employment, while rural voters focus more on agriculture-related issues and welfare schemes.

Methodology

The survey was conducted between May 15 and June 5, 2023, covering 100,000 respondents across 500 constituencies in 6 states. The sample included representation from all age groups, socio-economic categories, and both urban and rural areas.

The margin of error is ±3% at the state level and ±5% at the constituency level, with a 95% confidence level.

As the election approaches, these numbers may shift based on campaign strategies, candidate selections, and emerging issues, making this one of the most closely watched elections in recent history.
